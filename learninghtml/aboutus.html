At Moloko's livestock we sell all types of byproducts from our animals such as:
1. Cows: Milk: Used for dairy products like cheese, butter, yogurt, and ice cream. Leather: Tanned and processed into goods like clothing, shoes, or furniture. Manure: Used as fertilizer for crops or sold to other farms. Beef: Sold as meat to markets, stores, or butcher shops.
2. chickens Eggs: Sold for consumption or hatching. Feathers: Can be used in down products (like pillows and jackets) or for crafting materials. Manure: A high-nutrient fertilizer for crops. Meat (Broilers): Sold for consumption as chicken meat.
3. Pigs: Byproducts: Pork: Sold as fresh cuts of meat, sausages, or bacon. Manure: Used for fertilizing crops or sold. Leather: Pigskin can be processed into leather goods. Lard: Used in cooking or as a byproduct for soap making.
4. Sheep: Byproducts: Wool: Used for textiles, such as clothing or blankets. Milk: Sometimes used to make cheeses (e.g., feta, ricotta). Manure: Used as fertilizer for crops. Meat (Lamb/Mutton): Sold for consumption.
5. Goats: Byproducts: Milk: Used to make cheeses like goat cheese or yogurt. Meat (Goat Meat or Chevon): Sold as a source of protein. Leather: Processed into durable leather goods. Manure: Often sold as a fertilizer.
